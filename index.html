About Me

I'm a first-year MS student in Computer Science at the University of Nebraska–Lincoln on the Option A thesis track, planning to graduate Spring 2027. My academic journey started as an undergraduate at UNL where I graduated with a BS in Computer Science, a Minor in Mathematics, and High Distinction honors — while simultaneously spending four years as a teaching assistant and learning how students actually learn to code.

That teaching experience is what drives my research. I've watched students get falsely accused of AI use by tools that couldn't tell a strong formal writer from a language model. I've watched neurodivergent children locked out of expressive communication because AI systems were never trained on people like them. I build things to fix that.

I attended the CMU IT Lab Summer Security Intensive in 2025, training in network defense, information security, and cyber intelligence. I'm a founding student representative of UNL's reactivated Upsilon Pi Epsilon chapter — the first active CS honor society at UNL in over 20 years. I'm applying to PhD programs in HCI and AI for Fall 2027 with the goal of becoming a professor and researcher.

Research & Projects

01
MS Thesis · In Progress · 2026–2027

Multi-Modal AI Content Detection Framework

A unified detection system spanning three modalities — written text, source code, and images — that identifies AI-generated content using forensic signals grounded in the detection literature. Achieves a zero false positive rate on human-written text and 89.8% true positive rate on source code. Features a false positive reduction layer protecting students from erroneous accusations, and an assignment context module — novel among published systems — detecting mismatches between what a teacher assigned and what was submitted. Fine-tuned RoBERTa and CodeBERT transformers replace the API-dependent engine for free classroom use.

03
In Progress · Conference Submission Planned

Emotionally-Aware AAC System for Nonverbal Neurodivergent Children

Real-time pipeline detecting emotion from facial expressions using a custom CNN, then modulating text-to-speech prosody so synthesized speech sounds emotionally expressive rather than robotic — giving nonverbal children richer communication. A deliberate architectural decision collapsed Fear and Disgust into Neutral to reduce harmful misclassification for users whose expressions don't match neurotypical training data. Includes a fine-tuning pipeline for personalization to individual children over time.
