About Me

I am a Computer Science student at the University of Nebraska–Lincoln, focusing my work at the intersection of human-centered software and intelligent systems. My path in computer science is deeply tied to mentorship; serving as a Teaching Assistant for three different CSE classes at UNL has completely shaped how I see technology and code. Watching how real people interact with systems, hit roadblocks, and learn to solve problems is what inspires me to build better tools.

This hands-on teaching perspective drives my research questions. I am passionate about ensuring that AI systems treat users fairly, especially when evaluating human work or intent. My goal is to build intelligent platforms that avoid false assumptions, respect individual cognitive differences, and empower rather than exclude underrepresented or neurodivergent communities.

In the summer of 2025, I participated in the Carnegie Mellon University IT Lab Summer Security Intensive, where I built a solid foundation in network defense and information security. When I'm not debugging models or working with students, you can usually find me building clean frontends in React, designing custom layouts, or diving into technical projects that merge design with logic. I am currently preparing for PhD programs in HCI and AI to dive deeper into these research frontiers.

An implementation and analysis tool centering on the BM25 retrieval algorithm. The system evaluates document collections by analyzing term frequency, document lengths, and inverse document frequency across varying query types. Explores the explicit behavioral differences between short keyword searches and long sentence-based conversational queries to optimize ranking precision and text relevance scores.
